Standard Service

My standard service consists of sharpening on the 220 grit stone, grading up to 1000 grit, and finishing with a hone and a hand wash. After washing and drying the knife, I place your knife in its sheath or a cardboard sheath cut to length. Most knives will be eligible for this service, with the exception of speciality knives (ex. a single bevel Japanese knife).

Our Process

I start your knife off with a 220 grit grind to clean it and set the angle, then grade up to 1000 grits before honing the blade to remove the burr (the almost microscopic roll of the edge created during sharpening). I then handwash the knife and prepare it to return to its owner.

I do all of my sharpening on a Tormek T8 for two main reasons:

  • In a skilled hand, it provides an unparalleled edge on a wide range of blades
  • The stone constantly runs through a trough of water to prevent the blade from overheating, which can cause damage to the temper of your edge. This is an issue with bench grinders and belt sanders that makes your edge more brittle, increasing its likeliness to chip.
